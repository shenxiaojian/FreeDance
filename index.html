FreeDance: Dance Video Generation Conditioned on Music and Text

Jianrong Zhang1   Hehe Fan2   Xiaojian Shen3   Yi Yang2  

1 University of Technology Sydney   2 Zhejiang University   3 Jilin University  

Abstract

Thanks to the success of generative models (e.g., GAN and diffusion models), generating videos from music has achieved significant progress.However, existing models largely ignore the capability to flexibly synthesize videos based on textual instructions. In this work, we introduce a new task that generates customized dance videos from a piece of music and textual instruction, where the music indicates the human motion and the text describes the video content.To address the problem, we propose a simple yet effective music-text-to-video generation framework based on the human motion generative model and the recent breakthrough text-to-image diffusion models.Specifically, for motion generation, we use a Vector Quantised Variational AutoEncoder (VQ-VAE) to learn the discrete representation of motions and employ a Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT) to generate discrete representations in an auto-regressive manner conditioned on music. For video generation, we combine a text-to-image generation model (i.e., Stable Diffusion) and signal-conditioned control models (i.e., ControlNet) to synthesize the desired frames.Moreover, we find that incorporating the depth map and skeleton of the motion enables the model to synthesize a better foreground human pose and a more reasonable background circumstance. Extensive experiments on the AIST++ dataset demonstrate that our approach facilitates the personalization and customization of music-text-to-video generation.
